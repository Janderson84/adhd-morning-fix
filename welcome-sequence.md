## EMAIL 2 — DAY 1 (next morning)

**Subject:** The one thing that makes the checklist actually work

Hey {first_name},

A lot of ADHD parenting advice boils down to "be more consistent." Which is about as helpful as telling someone with a broken leg to "walk better."

Here's what actually makes morning systems stick — and it's probably the opposite of what you've been told:

**Don't nag. Point.**

Here's what I mean.

When your kid is staring at a wall instead of getting dressed, the instinct is to say: "Come on, we're going to be late. Get dressed. I asked you three times already."

The ADHD brain hears: noise. Pressure. Shame.

Instead, try this: walk over to the checklist. Point at step 2. Say nothing. Wait three seconds.

Here's why this works: you've removed the verbal processing demand. You've made it visual. You've made it *the chart's* instruction, not *your* instruction. They're following the system — not you.

The Parent Setup Guide calls this the "don't nag" method. It's one of the most counterintuitive things about parenting an ADHD kid, and it's probably the thing that will save your mornings.

Try it tomorrow. Point, don't speak. See what happens.

James

P.S. If they still ignore it? That's normal — and page 5 of the Setup Guide has the "ignore protocol." It's a one-sentence response that resets the dynamic without a fight.

They're not supposed to work every day. They're supposed to work *most* days — and the difference between a good week and a bad week is what you do on the day they don't.

That's why The ADHD Morning Fix includes the Bad Morning Protocol.

It's one page. It has three steps:

1. **Reset, don't replay.** Don't re-litigate the morning. "OK, that one got away. We'll try again tomorrow." That's it.

2. **Remove one barrier tonight.** Did they not get dressed because they couldn't find clean clothes? Did they skip breakfast because the cereal was on a high shelf? ADHD brains get blocked by tiny friction points. Find one and remove it.

3. **Tomorrow is new.** No punishment. No "we're still mad about this morning." No residue. ADHD kids carry enough shame. The system resets at midnight.

Most parenting products give you the tool. They don't tell you what to do when the tool doesn't work today.
