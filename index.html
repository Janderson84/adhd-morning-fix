The ADHD Morning Fix isn't another pretty chart. It's built on three things ADHD researchers and occupational therapists know — but most parenting products ignore:

1

The 5-Step Maximum

ADHD brains get overwhelmed by too many choices. Most checklists have 8–12 steps. Ours has 5. That's it. Get up. Get dressed. Eat. Brush teeth. Grab bag. When every step is visible at once, there's no "what's next?" paralysis.

2

Visual Completion, Not Verbal Reminders

ADHD kids tune out verbal instructions within seconds. (You've noticed.) The checklist uses completion cues the ADHD brain actually registers — checkmarks, colors, a done state. No parent narration required.

3

The Bad Morning Protocol

Every system breaks. The difference is whether you recover or spiral. The Bad Morning Protocol is a one-page reset that says: this morning went wrong. Here's what we do now. Tomorrow is new. No shame. No "we failed again." Just a re-set.
